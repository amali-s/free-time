# FreeTime Impact: How It Solves Real Scenarios

## Ava — Traveler with a Laptop, No Car, Low Battery

**The Problem:**
Ava lands in Denver at 10am with a 50% battery, work to complete, and hotel check-in not until 3pm. She's in an unfamiliar city with no car and no way to quickly find a suitable working space.

**How FreeTime Solves It:**

| Step | Without FreeTime | With FreeTime |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Find a space** | Search Google Maps, browse Yelp reviews (unclear if they have wifi/outlets), call cafes to ask | Open FreeTime, type "Denver", instantly see filtered cards with wifi + outlet tags |
| **Location context** | Unfamiliar with neighborhoods, can't judge proximity | Spots cafe in the same neighborhood as her hotel (recognizes the name) |
| **Navigate** | Figure out transit options from airport | Copies address directly, routes via Google Maps — only 2 blocks from hotel |
| **Outcome** | 45+ minutes of searching, uncertainty | 5 minutes to find + book, confidence in her choice |

**Impact:**
- **Battery saved**: Didn't waste battery searching. Charged while working.
- **Time regained**: 3 hours of uninterrupted work instead of scrambling.
- **Stress eliminated**: Knew exactly where to go and what amenities awaited.
- **Discovery bonus**: Found a cafe she'll likely return to on future visits.

## Brendon — New in Town, Office Closed, Meetings in 15 Minutes

**The Problem:**
Brendon's office is unexpectedly closed. He has a virtual meeting in 15 minutes, needs a quiet place with wifi, and has no idea what's nearby in Austin. Zero time to plan.

**How FreeTime Solves It:**

| Step | Without FreeTime | With FreeTime |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Enable geolocation** | N/A (would need to manually search "Austin near me") | Taps "Allow location", instantly shows spaces around him in the Domain area |
| **Filter to requirements** | Browse all Austin cafes, call 5+ places to ask about wifi/noise | Filters: seating + wifi. Only sees relevant options. |
| **Find a free/cheap option** | Scan prices across search results, calculate costs | Price labels visible on cards; avoids paid coworking spaces |
| **Evaluate & pick** | Read mixed reviews, still uncertain | Reads "Capital One cafe — moderately quiet" description and decides confidently |
| **Navigate** | Use GPS, hope for good traffic | Google Maps routing shows 10-minute walk |
| **Arrive prepared** | 8 minutes left before meeting | 5 minutes early, calm, ready |

**Impact:**
- **Time saved**: 10 minutes of frantic searching avoided. Actually early to the meeting.
- **Stress reduced**: No ambiguity about noise level or amenities — description is clear.
- **Professional outcome**: Takes his call in a quiet cafe, not in his car or a noisy street.
- **Cost avoided**: Avoids wasting money on premium coworking day-pass when a cafe works fine.

## Alex — Traveler with Luggage, Exploring a New City

**The Problem:**
Alex arrives in Bologna 4 hours before hotel check-in. He has a carry-on he needs to store, wants to explore freely, and has a budget of $10. He's in a small city he doesn't know.

**How FreeTime Solves It:**

| Step | Without FreeTime | With FreeTime |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Find storage** | Search "luggage storage Bologna" in Italian, find confusing options, call hotels | Open FreeTime, allows geolocation (train station area), filters by "Storage" tag |
| **Check prices** | No prices listed online; must call or visit in person | Card display shows price upfront in description; can compare without leaving train station |
| **Evaluate options** | Train station storage: calls, asks price ($5/hour = too expensive) | Sees train station option in card view, reads "$5/hour" in description — eliminates it immediately |
| **Find alternative** | Wander city, ask staff, get lost, waste time | Continues scrolling filtered list, finds hotel-area storage: "$8 one-time fee" — within budget |
| **Navigate & execute** | Get directions from phone, may get lost | Routes to the storage location via Google Maps |
| **Outcome** | 90 minutes lost to logistics | 10 minutes, luggage stored, 3.5 hours free to explore |

**Impact:**
- **Time liberated**: 4 hours of city exploration instead of 2 hours.
- **Budget protected**: Compared prices before committing; saved money vs. impulse paying.
- **Confidence**: Knew exact pricing upfront — no surprises at the counter.
- **Experience**: Enjoys Bologna freely instead of worrying about luggage.

## Key Takeaways

| Persona | Core Need | FreeTime Solves |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Ava** | "Where can I work for 3 hours?" | Filters by amenities (wifi + outlets), shows proximity to hotel, saves battery and time |
| **Brendon** | "Where can I take a call in 15 minutes?" | Geolocation + instant filtering by amenity + noise level descriptor = zero-friction decision |
| **Alex** | "Where can I store my luggage affordably?" | Filters by storage amenity, shows pricing upfront, eliminates search time in unfamiliar city |

**Common Pattern:**
All three solved their problem in **5–10 minutes** using FreeTime, compared to **45+ minutes** of searching, calling, and uncertainty without it. They all moved confidently to the location and achieved their goal—work, calls, or exploration—without friction.
